The manufacturing sector has undergone significant transformations in recent years, driven by technological advancements and the need for enhanced efficiency. One critical area of focus has been the adoption of Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment (CI/CD) pipelines to expedite the software development lifecycle. However, many manufacturing companies are hindered by legacy testing frameworks that create bottlenecks in their CI/CD pipelines. In this blog post, we'll explore how Okta can help manufacturing organizations overcome these bottlenecks and achieve streamlined CI/CD pipelines.

#### The Challenge of Legacy Testing Frameworks
Legacy testing frameworks are outdated and inflexible, making them a significant obstacle to efficient CI/CD pipelines. These frameworks often rely on manual testing, which is time-consuming and prone to errors. Moreover, they may not be compatible with modern development tools and technologies, leading to integration challenges and increased maintenance costs. As a result, manufacturing companies are forced to spend more time and resources on testing, rather than focusing on innovation and delivering high-quality products to market.

#### The Impact on Manufacturing
The manufacturing sector is particularly susceptible to the challenges posed by legacy testing frameworks. With complex supply chains, stringent regulatory requirements, and high-pressure production schedules, manufacturers cannot afford to be held back by inefficient testing processes. The consequences of delayed or flawed product releases can be severe, resulting in lost revenue, damaged brand reputation, and compromised customer satisfaction. Therefore, it is essential for manufacturing companies to address the bottlenecks in their CI/CD pipelines and adopt modern, agile testing approaches.
