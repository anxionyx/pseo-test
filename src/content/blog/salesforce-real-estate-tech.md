### Solving the Lack of Unified Audit Logs for Microservices Orchestration in Real Estate Tech with Salesforce
The real estate technology (Real Estate Tech) industry has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the increasing demand for digital solutions to enhance the home buying and selling experience. As a result, many real estate companies have adopted microservices architecture to improve the scalability and flexibility of their applications. However, this shift has also introduced new challenges, particularly when it comes to auditing and logging. In this blog post, we will explore the issue of lack of unified audit logs for microservices orchestration in Real Estate Tech and how Salesforce can help solve this problem.

#### The Challenge of Microservices Orchestration
Microservices architecture involves breaking down a large application into smaller, independent services that communicate with each other through APIs. While this approach offers many benefits, such as improved scalability and fault tolerance, it also creates new challenges when it comes to monitoring and auditing. With multiple services interacting with each other, it can be difficult to track and log all the activities across the system. This lack of visibility can make it challenging to detect and respond to security incidents, troubleshoot issues, and comply with regulatory requirements.

#### The Importance of Unified Audit Logs
Unified audit logs are essential for real estate companies to ensure the security, integrity, and compliance of their systems. Audit logs provide a record of all activities across the system, including user interactions, system changes, and data access. With unified audit logs, companies can:

* Detect and respond to security incidents in a timely manner
* Troubleshoot issues and identify root causes
* Comply with regulatory requirements, such as GDPR and HIPAA
* Improve overall system security and integrity
