# Solving the Lack of Unified Audit Logs for Microservices Orchestration in GovTech with Notion
===========================================================

As governments increasingly adopt microservices architecture to modernize their systems and improve citizen services, they face a new challenge: the lack of unified audit logs. This issue can hinder the ability to track, monitor, and secure the interactions between various microservices, ultimately compromising the integrity of the entire system. In this blog post, we will explore how Notion can help GovTech agencies overcome this challenge and ensure the security and compliance of their microservices orchestration.

## The Problem: Lack of Unified Audit Logs
----------------------------------------

Microservices architecture is a distributed system, where multiple services communicate with each other to achieve a common goal. Each service generates its own logs, which can lead to a fragmented and disjointed logging system. This makes it difficult for GovTech agencies to:

* Track and monitor interactions between services
* Identify and respond to security incidents
* Ensure compliance with regulatory requirements
* Analyze and optimize system performance

## The Solution: Notion's Unified Audit Logs
-----------------------------------------

* **Standardize log format**: Normalize log data to ensure consistency and ease of analysis
* **Monitor and track interactions**: Visualize service interactions and identify potential security risks
* **Analyze and optimize performance**: Use log data to identify bottlenecks and optimize system performance

--------------------

Notion's unified audit logs are made possible through its flexible and customizable data model. Agencies can create a customized log template to collect and store log data from all microservices. This template can include fields such as:
