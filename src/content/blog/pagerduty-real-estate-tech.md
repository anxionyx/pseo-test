### Solving the Lack of Unified Audit Logs for Microservices Orchestration in Real Estate Tech
The real estate technology (PropTech) industry has experienced significant growth in recent years, driven by the adoption of digital technologies to improve the way properties are bought, sold, and managed. As PropTech companies continue to innovate and expand their services, they often turn to microservices architecture to increase scalability, flexibility, and resilience. However, this shift towards microservices also introduces new challenges, particularly when it comes to monitoring and auditing these distributed systems. One of the critical issues faced by PropTech companies is the lack of unified audit logs for microservices orchestration.

#### The Challenge of Microservices Orchestration
Microservices architecture involves breaking down a large application into smaller, independent services that communicate with each other. While this approach offers numerous benefits, including improved fault tolerance and easier maintenance, it also creates complexity. Each microservice may have its own logging mechanism, resulting in a plethora of logs that are difficult to manage and analyze. This makes it challenging for PropTech companies to have a unified view of their system's performance, security, and compliance.

#### The Need for Unified Audit Logs
Unified audit logs are essential for PropTech companies to ensure the integrity, security, and compliance of their systems. These logs provide a centralized record of all activities across the microservices, allowing for better monitoring, debugging, and auditing. With unified audit logs, PropTech companies can:

* **Enhance Security**: Quickly identify and respond to security incidents by analyzing logs for suspicious activity.
* **Improve Compliance**: Demonstrate compliance with regulatory requirements by maintaining accurate and detailed logs of system activities.
* **Optimize Performance**: Identify bottlenecks and areas for improvement by analyzing log data on system performance and user behavior.
