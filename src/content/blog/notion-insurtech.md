# Unified Audit Logs for InsurTech: Streamlining Microservices Orchestration with Notion
The InsurTech industry has witnessed a significant surge in the adoption of microservices architecture, enabling companies to develop and deploy applications more efficiently. However, this shift has also introduced new challenges, particularly when it comes to auditing and logging. In this post, we'll explore how Notion can help InsurTech companies overcome the lack of unified audit logs for microservices orchestration.

## The Challenge of Microservices Orchestration
Microservices architecture involves breaking down a monolithic application into smaller, independent services that communicate with each other. While this approach offers numerous benefits, such as increased scalability and flexibility, it also creates complexity when it comes to auditing and logging. With multiple services generating logs, it can be difficult to track and monitor activities across the entire system.

## The Importance of Unified Audit Logs
Unified audit logs are essential for InsurTech companies to ensure compliance, security, and transparency. They provide a single source of truth for all system activities, enabling companies to:

* Monitor and detect potential security threats
* Investigate incidents and perform root cause analysis
* Meet regulatory requirements and compliance standards
* Optimize system performance and improve debugging

## The Limitations of Traditional Logging Solutions
Traditional logging solutions often fall short in providing unified audit logs for microservices orchestration. They may:

* Only capture logs from individual services, making it difficult to correlate activities across the system
* Lack real-time monitoring and alerting capabilities
* Require significant manual configuration and maintenance
* Fail to provide a centralized and searchable log repository
